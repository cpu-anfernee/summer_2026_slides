Chapter 5.8

Inverse Trigonometric Functions

Integration Rules Yielding Arcsine, Arctangent, and Arcsecant

The Three Standard Integration Rules

When integrating rational or radical functions that do not fit the standard Power or Log rules, look for these three distinct patterns (where \( a > 0 \)):

1. Inverse Sine (Arcsine)

\[ \int \frac{du}{\sqrt{a^2 - u^2}} = \arcsin\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + C \]

2. Inverse Tangent (Arctangent)

\[ \int \frac{du}{a^2 + u^2} = \frac{1}{a} \arctan\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + C \]

3. Inverse Secant (Arcsecant)

\[ \int \frac{du}{u\sqrt{u^2 - a^2}} = \frac{1}{a} \text{arcsec}\left(\frac{|u|}{a}\right) + C \]

Notice that only arctangent and arcsecant have the \( \frac{1}{a} \) coefficient in front!

Example 1: Basic Arcsine

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{dx}{\sqrt{16 - x^2}} \)

  1. Identify the Pattern: The denominator has a square root with a constant minus a variable squared. This matches the arcsine form.
  2. Identify \( a \) and \( u \):
    \( a^2 = 16 \implies a = 4 \)
    \( u^2 = x^2 \implies u = x \)
  3. Find \( du \):
    \( du = dx \)
  4. Apply the Rule:
    \( \int \frac{du}{\sqrt{a^2 - u^2}} = \arcsin\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + C \)
  5. Final Answer:
    \( \arcsin\left(\frac{x}{4}\right) + C \)

Example 2: Arctangent with u-Substitution

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{dx}{2 + 9x^2} \)

  1. Identify the Pattern: No square root, sum of two squares in the denominator. This is the arctangent form.
  2. Identify \( a \) and \( u \):
    \( a^2 = 2 \implies a = \sqrt{2} \)
    \( u^2 = 9x^2 \implies u = 3x \)
  3. Find \( du \):
    \( du = 3 dx \). We only have \( dx \), so divide by 3: \( dx = \frac{du}{3} \)
  4. Substitute:
    \( \int \frac{du/3}{a^2 + u^2} = \frac{1}{3} \int \frac{du}{a^2 + u^2} \)
  5. Final Answer: Apply the \( \frac{1}{a} \) coefficient.
    \( \frac{1}{3} \left[ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \arctan\left(\frac{3x}{\sqrt{2}}\right) \right] + C = \frac{1}{3\sqrt{2}} \arctan\left(\frac{3x}{\sqrt{2}}\right) + C \)

Example 3: Arcsecant Manipulation

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{dx}{x\sqrt{4x^2 - 9}} \)

  1. Identify the Pattern: Variable outside the square root, and variable squared minus a constant inside. This is the arcsecant form.
  2. Identify \( a \) and \( u \):
    \( a^2 = 9 \implies a = 3 \)
    \( u^2 = 4x^2 \implies u = 2x \)
  3. Adjust the Denominator: We need a \( u \) (which is \( 2x \)) outside the root, but we only have \( x \). Multiply top and bottom by 2.
    \( \int \frac{2 dx}{2x\sqrt{(2x)^2 - 3^2}} \)
  4. Find \( du \):
    \( u = 2x \implies du = 2 dx \). Notice that our numerator is exactly \( du \)!
  5. Substitute:
    \( \int \frac{du}{u\sqrt{u^2 - a^2}} \)
  6. Final Answer:
    \( \frac{1}{3} \text{arcsec}\left(\frac{|2x|}{3}\right) + C \)

Example 4: Completing the Square

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{dx}{x^2 - 4x + 7} \)

If the denominator is an unfactorable quadratic, complete the square to force the arctangent form!

  1. Complete the Square: Take half the middle term (-4/2 = -2) and square it (4). Add and subtract this inside the denominator.
    \( x^2 - 4x + 4 - 4 + 7 \)
    \( (x - 2)^2 + 3 \)
  2. Rewrite the Integral:
    \( \int \frac{dx}{(x - 2)^2 + 3} \)
  3. Identify \( a \) and \( u \):
    \( u^2 = (x - 2)^2 \implies u = x - 2 \) and \( du = dx \)
    \( a^2 = 3 \implies a = \sqrt{3} \)
  4. Final Answer:
    \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \arctan\left(\frac{x - 2}{\sqrt{3}}\right) + C \)

Example 5: Splitting the Fraction

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{x + 2}{\sqrt{9 - x^2}} dx \)

  1. Split the Numerator: We cannot use a simple u-sub because of the "+ 2". Separate it into two integrals.
    \( \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{9 - x^2}} dx + \int \frac{2}{\sqrt{9 - x^2}} dx \)
  2. Solve Integral 1 (Power Rule): Let \( w = 9 - x^2 \), so \( dw = -2x dx \).
    \( \int x(9 - x^2)^{-1/2} dx = -\frac{1}{2} \int w^{-1/2} dw = -\frac{1}{2} (2w^{1/2}) = -\sqrt{9 - x^2} \)
  3. Solve Integral 2 (Arcsine): Pull out the 2. \( a = 3, u = x \).
    \( 2 \int \frac{dx}{\sqrt{3^2 - x^2}} = 2 \arcsin\left(\frac{x}{3}\right) \)
  4. Final Answer: Combine the results.
    \( -\sqrt{9 - x^2} + 2 \arcsin\left(\frac{x}{3}\right) + C \)
