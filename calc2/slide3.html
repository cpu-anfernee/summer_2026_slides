Chapter 4.5

Integration by Substitution

Pattern Recognition & Change of Variables

Part 1: Pattern Recognition

In this technique, we perform the Chain Rule in reverse. We look for an integral that fits the following pattern:

\[ \int f(g(x)) \cdot g^{\prime}(x) dx = F(g(x)) + C \]
  • \( g(x) \) is the "inner" function.
  • \( g^{\prime}(x) \) is the exact derivative of the inner function, multiplying the whole expression.
  • \( F \) is the antiderivative of the "outer" function \( f \).

Pattern Recognition: Example 1

Evaluate: \( \int 2x(x^2 + 1)^4 dx \)

  1. Identify the Pattern:
    Inner function \( g(x) = x^2 + 1 \).
    Outer function is \( (\text{something})^4 \).
    Derivative of inner is \( g^{\prime}(x) = 2x \).
  2. Verify: We have exactly \( g^{\prime}(x) \) sitting outside! The pattern is perfect.
  3. Integrate the Outer Function: Use the power rule on \( (\text{something})^4 \).
    \( \int u^4 du = \frac{u^5}{5} \)
  4. Final Answer:
    \( = \frac{1}{5}(x^2 + 1)^5 + C \)

Pattern Recognition: Example 2

Evaluate: \( \int (3x^2 + 2)(x^3 + 2x)^5 dx \)

  1. Identify the Pattern:
    Inner function \( g(x) = x^3 + 2x \).
    Derivative \( g^{\prime}(x) = 3x^2 + 2 \).
  2. Verify: The expression \( (3x^2 + 2) \) is perfectly matched.
  3. Integrate the Outer Function: Apply the power rule to the exponent 5.
    \( \int (\text{inner})^5 \cdot (\text{deriv}) dx = \frac{(\text{inner})^6}{6} \)
  4. Final Answer:
    \( = \frac{1}{6}(x^3 + 2x)^6 + C \)

Pattern Recognition: Example 3

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{4x^3 + 1}{(x^4 + x)^2} dx \)

  1. Rewrite: Bring the denominator up using a negative exponent.
    \( \int (4x^3 + 1)(x^4 + x)^{-2} dx \)
  2. Identify the Pattern:
    Inner \( g(x) = x^4 + x \).
    Derivative \( g^{\prime}(x) = 4x^3 + 1 \). Perfect match.
  3. Integrate: Add 1 to the power (-2 + 1 = -1) and divide by -1.
    \( = \frac{(x^4 + x)^{-1}}{-1} + C \)
  4. Final Answer:
    \( = -\frac{1}{x^4 + x} + C \)

Pattern Recognition: Example 4 (Off by a Constant)

Evaluate: \( \int x^2(x^3 - 5)^7 dx \)

Notice: We have the right variables, but the wrong constant number!

  1. Identify Inner & Derivative: \( g(x) = x^3 - 5 \), so \( g^{\prime}(x) = 3x^2 \).
  2. Adjust the Constant: We only have \( x^2 \). We need \( 3x^2 \).
    Multiply inside by 3, and outside by \( \frac{1}{3} \) to balance it:
    \( = \frac{1}{3} \int (3x^2)(x^3 - 5)^7 dx \)
  3. Integrate: Now the pattern is perfect inside the integral.
    \( = \frac{1}{3} \left[ \frac{(x^3 - 5)^8}{8} \right] + C \)
  4. Final Answer:
    \( = \frac{1}{24}(x^3 - 5)^8 + C \)

Pattern Recognition: Example 5

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2 + 9}} dx \)

  1. Rewrite: \( \int x(x^2 + 9)^{-1/2} dx \)
  2. Identify & Adjust: \( g(x) = x^2 + 9 \), so \( g^{\prime}(x) = 2x \). We only have \( x \).
    Multiply inside by 2, outside by \( \frac{1}{2} \):
    \( = \frac{1}{2} \int 2x(x^2 + 9)^{-1/2} dx \)
  3. Integrate: Power rule on \( -1/2 \). Add 1 to get \( 1/2 \), divide by \( 1/2 \).
    \( = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \frac{(x^2 + 9)^{1/2}}{1/2} \right] + C \)
  4. Final Answer: The \( \frac{1}{2} \) and dividing by \( 1/2 \) cancel out.
    \( = \sqrt{x^2 + 9} + C \)

Pattern Recognition: Example 6

Evaluate: \( \int \sin(3x) dx \)

  1. Identify & Adjust: Inner \( g(x) = 3x \). Derivative is 3. We are missing a 3.
    Multiply inside by 3, outside by \( \frac{1}{3} \).
    \( = \frac{1}{3} \int \sin(3x) \cdot 3 dx \)
  2. Integrate: The antiderivative of sine is negative cosine.
    \( = \frac{1}{3} (-\cos(3x)) + C \)
  3. Final Answer:
    \( = -\frac{1}{3}\cos(3x) + C \)

Part 2: Change of Variables (Formal u-Substitution)

Sometimes, the derivative is NOT present, and adjusting by a constant won't fix it. We must completely rewrite the integral in terms of a new variable, \( u \).

  1. Let \( u = g(x) \).
  2. Find \( du = g^{\prime}(x)dx \).
  3. If there are leftover \( x \)'s, solve \( u = g(x) \) for \( x \) and substitute!
  4. Integrate with respect to \( u \).
  5. Substitute \( x \) back in.

(Press Down Arrow for 6 Examples)

Change of Variables: Example 1

Evaluate: \( \int x\sqrt{x + 1} dx \)

Why PR fails: Derivative of (x+1) is 1. We have an extra 'x' outside!

  1. Set up \( u \): Let \( u = x + 1 \). Therefore, \( du = dx \).
  2. Solve for leftover \( x \): Since \( u = x + 1 \), then \( x = u - 1 \).
  3. Substitute everything to \( u \):
    \( \int (u - 1)\sqrt{u} du = \int (u - 1)u^{1/2} du \)
  4. Distribute & Integrate:
    \( = \int (u^{3/2} - u^{1/2}) du \)
    \( = \frac{2}{5}u^{5/2} - \frac{2}{3}u^{3/2} + C \)
  5. Final Answer (Substitute back):
    \( = \frac{2}{5}(x + 1)^{5/2} - \frac{2}{3}(x + 1)^{3/2} + C \)

Change of Variables: Example 2

Evaluate: \( \int x^2\sqrt{1 - x} dx \)

  1. Set up \( u \): Let \( u = 1 - x \). Then \( du = -dx \), so \( dx = -du \).
  2. Solve for leftover \( x^2 \): Since \( x = 1 - u \), then \( x^2 = (1 - u)^2 = 1 - 2u + u^2 \).
  3. Substitute:
    \( \int (1 - 2u + u^2)\sqrt{u}(-du) = -\int (u^{1/2} - 2u^{3/2} + u^{5/2}) du \)
  4. Integrate:
    \( = -\left( \frac{2}{3}u^{3/2} - 2(\frac{2}{5})u^{5/2} + \frac{2}{7}u^{7/2} \right) + C \)
  5. Final Answer:
    \( = -\frac{2}{3}(1 - x)^{3/2} + \frac{4}{5}(1 - x)^{5/2} - \frac{2}{7}(1 - x)^{7/2} + C \)

Change of Variables: Example 3

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{x}{(x - 1)^3} dx \)

  1. Set up \( u \): Let \( u = x - 1 \). Then \( du = dx \).
  2. Solve for leftover \( x \): \( x = u + 1 \).
  3. Substitute:
    \( \int \frac{u + 1}{u^3} du \)
  4. Divide and Integrate:
    \( = \int (u^{-2} + u^{-3}) du \)
    \( = \frac{u^{-1}}{-1} + \frac{u^{-2}}{-2} + C \)
    \( = -\frac{1}{u} - \frac{1}{2u^2} + C \)
  5. Final Answer:
    \( = -\frac{1}{x - 1} - \frac{1}{2(x - 1)^2} + C \)

Change of Variables: Example 4

Evaluate: \( \int x\sqrt{2x - 1} dx \)

  1. Set up \( u \): Let \( u = 2x - 1 \). Then \( du = 2dx \), so \( dx = \frac{du}{2} \).
  2. Solve for leftover \( x \): \( 2x = u + 1 \implies x = \frac{u + 1}{2} \).
  3. Substitute:
    \( \int \left(\frac{u + 1}{2}\right) \sqrt{u} \left(\frac{du}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{4} \int (u + 1)u^{1/2} du \)
  4. Distribute & Integrate:
    \( = \frac{1}{4} \int (u^{3/2} + u^{1/2}) du = \frac{1}{4} \left( \frac{2}{5}u^{5/2} + \frac{2}{3}u^{3/2} \right) + C \)
  5. Final Answer:
    \( = \frac{1}{10}(2x - 1)^{5/2} + \frac{1}{6}(2x - 1)^{3/2} + C \)

Change of Variables: Example 5

Evaluate: \( \int \frac{2x + 1}{\sqrt{x + 4}} dx \)

  1. Set up \( u \): Let \( u = x + 4 \). Then \( du = dx \).
  2. Solve for leftover \( x \): \( x = u - 4 \).
  3. Substitute (Numerator becomes \( 2(u-4)+1 \)):
    \( \int \frac{2u - 8 + 1}{\sqrt{u}} du = \int \frac{2u - 7}{u^{1/2}} du \)
  4. Divide & Integrate:
    \( = \int (2u^{1/2} - 7u^{-1/2}) du \)
    \( = 2\left(\frac{2}{3}u^{3/2}\right) - 7\left(2u^{1/2}\right) + C \)
  5. Final Answer:
    \( = \frac{4}{3}(x + 4)^{3/2} - 14\sqrt{x + 4} + C \)

Change of Variables: Example 6

Evaluate: \( \int (x + 1)\sqrt{2 - x} dx \)

  1. Set up \( u \): Let \( u = 2 - x \). Then \( du = -dx \), so \( dx = -du \).
  2. Solve for leftover \( x \): \( x = 2 - u \).
  3. Substitute (Factor becomes \( (2-u)+1 = 3-u \)):
    \( \int (3 - u)\sqrt{u}(-du) = \int (u - 3)u^{1/2} du \)
  4. Distribute & Integrate:
    \( = \int (u^{3/2} - 3u^{1/2}) du \)
    \( = \frac{2}{5}u^{5/2} - 3\left(\frac{2}{3}u^{3/2}\right) + C \)
  5. Final Answer:
    \( = \frac{2}{5}(2 - x)^{5/2} - 2(2 - x)^{3/2} + C \)
