Bernoulli's Equation

Substitution for Non-Linear Equations

The Bernoulli Form

A Bernoulli differential equation is a non-linear equation of the form:

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x)y^n \]

The Transformation Strategy:

  1. Divide the entire equation by \( y^n \).
  2. Substitute \( v = y^{1-n} \).
  3. The resulting equation for \( v \) will be linear:
    \[ \frac{dv}{dx} + (1-n)P(x)v = (1-n)Q(x) \]

Example 1: \( n=2 \)

Solve: \( \frac{dy}{dx} + y = xy^2 \)

  1. Divide by \( y^2 \): \( y^{-2} \frac{dy}{dx} + y^{-1} = x \).
  2. Substitute: Let \( v = y^{-1} \). Then \( \frac{dv}{dx} = -y^{-2} \frac{dy}{dx} \).
  3. Transform: \( -\frac{dv}{dx} + v = x \implies \frac{dv}{dx} - v = -x \).
  4. Integrating Factor: \( \mu(x) = e^{\int -1 dx} = e^{-x} \).
  5. Integrate: \( \frac{d}{dx}[v e^{-x}] = -x e^{-x} \implies v e^{-x} = x e^{-x} + e^{-x} + C \).
  6. Back-Substitute: \( v = x + 1 + Ce^x \). Since \( v = 1/y \):
    Final Answer: \( y = \frac{1}{x + 1 + Ce^x} \)

Example 2: \( n=3 \)

Solve: \( \frac{dy}{dx} + y = e^x y^3 \)

  1. Divide by \( y^3 \): \( y^{-3} \frac{dy}{dx} + y^{-2} = e^x \).
  2. Substitute: \( v = y^{-2} \implies \frac{dv}{dx} = -2y^{-3} \frac{dy}{dx} \).
  3. Transform: \( -\frac{1}{2} \frac{dv}{dx} + v = e^x \implies \frac{dv}{dx} - 2v = -2e^x \).
  4. Integrating Factor: \( \mu(x) = e^{-2x} \).
  5. Integrate: \( v e^{-2x} = \int -2e^{-x} dx = 2e^{-x} + C \).
  6. Final Answer: \( v = 2e^x + Ce^{2x} \implies y = \pm (2e^x + Ce^{2x})^{-1/2} \).

Example 3: \( n=1/2 \)

Solve: \( \frac{dy}{dx} + y = y^{1/2} \)

  1. Divide by \( y^{1/2} \): \( y^{-1/2} \frac{dy}{dx} + y^{1/2} = 1 \).
  2. Substitute: \( v = y^{1/2} \implies \frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{1}{2} y^{-1/2} \frac{dy}{dx} \).
  3. Transform: \( 2\frac{dv}{dx} + v = 1 \implies \frac{dv}{dx} + \frac{1}{2}v = \frac{1}{2} \).
  4. Integrating Factor: \( \mu(x) = e^{\int 0.5 dx} = e^{x/2} \).
  5. Integrate: \( \frac{d}{dx}[v e^{x/2}] = \frac{1}{2}e^{x/2} \implies v e^{x/2} = e^{x/2} + C \).
  6. Final Answer: \( v = 1 + Ce^{-x/2} \implies y = (1 + Ce^{-x/2})^2 \).

Example 4: Initial Value Problem

Solve: \( \frac{dy}{dx} - y = y^2 \), \( y(0) = 1/2 \)

  1. Divide by \( y^2 \): \( y^{-2} \frac{dy}{dx} - y^{-1} = 1 \).
  2. Substitute: \( v = y^{-1} \implies \frac{dv}{dx} = -y^{-2} \frac{dy}{dx} \).
  3. Transform: \( -\frac{dv}{dx} - v = 1 \implies \frac{dv}{dx} + v = -1 \).
  4. Integrating Factor: \( \mu(x) = e^x \).
  5. Integrate: \( v e^x = \int -e^x dx = -e^x + C \implies v = -1 + Ce^{-x} \).
  6. Apply IVP: \( y(0) = 1/2 \implies v(0) = 2 \).
    \( 2 = -1 + C(1) \implies C = 3 \).
  7. Final Answer: \( y = \frac{1}{3e^{-x} - 1} \).

Example 5: Algebraic Case

Solve: \( x \frac{dy}{dx} + y = y^2 \ln x \)

  1. Standard Form: \( \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{1}{x} y = \frac{\ln x}{x} y^2 \).
  2. Divide by \( y^2 \): \( y^{-2} \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{1}{x} y^{-1} = \frac{\ln x}{x} \).
  3. Substitute: \( v = y^{-1} \implies \frac{dv}{dx} = -y^{-2} \frac{dy}{dx} \).
  4. Transform: \( -\frac{dv}{dx} + \frac{1}{x} v = \frac{\ln x}{x} \implies \frac{dv}{dx} - \frac{1}{x} v = -\frac{\ln x}{x} \).
  5. Integrating Factor: \( \mu(x) = e^{-\int 1/x dx} = 1/x \).
  6. Integrate: \( \frac{d}{dx}[v/x] = -\frac{\ln x}{x^2} \implies v/x = \frac{\ln x}{x} + \frac{1}{x} + C \).
  7. Final Answer: \( v = \ln x + 1 + Cx \implies y = \frac{1}{\ln x + 1 + Cx} \).
