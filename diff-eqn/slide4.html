Chapter 2.4

Exact Differential Equations

Testing for Exactness and Finding the Potential Function

What is an Exact Equation?

A differential equation written in the differential form:
\[ M(x, y) dx + N(x, y) dy = 0 \] is an exact differential equation if there exists a function \( F(x, y) \) such that:

\[ \frac{\partial F}{\partial x} = M(x, y) \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{\partial F}{\partial y} = N(x, y) \]

The Test for Exactness

If \( M \) and \( N \) have continuous partial derivatives, the equation is exact if and only if:

\[ \frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x} \]

Method of Solution

  1. Test for exactness: Verify that \( M_y = N_x \).
  2. Integrate M: Integrate \( M(x, y) \) with respect to \( x \). Because \( y \) is treated as a constant, the constant of integration is a function of \( y \), say \( g(y) \).
    \[ F(x, y) = \int M(x, y) dx + g(y) \]
  3. Differentiate F: Take the partial derivative of your new \( F(x, y) \) with respect to \( y \) and set it equal to \( N(x, y) \).
    \[ \frac{\partial F}{\partial y} = N(x, y) \]
  4. Solve for g(y): Isolate \( g^{\prime}(y) \) and integrate it with respect to \( y \) to find \( g(y) \).
  5. Write the Solution: The general implicit solution is:
    \[ F(x, y) = C \]

Example 1: Basic Polynomial

Solve: \( (2xy) dx + (x^2 - 1) dy = 0 \)

  1. Test for Exactness:
    \( M = 2xy \implies M_y = 2x \)
    \( N = x^2 - 1 \implies N_x = 2x \)
    Since \( M_y = N_x \), it is exact.
  2. Integrate \( M \) w.r.t \( x \):
    \( F(x,y) = \int 2xy \, dx = x^2y + g(y) \)
  3. Differentiate \( F \) w.r.t \( y \) and equate to \( N \):
    \( F_y = x^2 + g^{\prime}(y) \)
    \( x^2 + g^{\prime}(y) = x^2 - 1 \)
  4. Solve for \( g(y) \):
    \( g^{\prime}(y) = -1 \implies g(y) = -y \)
  5. Final Solution:
    \( F(x,y) = x^2y - y = C \)

Example 2: Linear Terms

Solve: \( (2x + y) dx + (x - 2y) dy = 0 \)

  1. Test for Exactness:
    \( M = 2x + y \implies M_y = 1 \)
    \( N = x - 2y \implies N_x = 1 \)
    Exact!
  2. Integrate \( M \) w.r.t \( x \):
    \( F(x,y) = \int (2x + y) dx = x^2 + xy + g(y) \)
  3. Equate \( F_y \) to \( N \):
    \( F_y = x + g^{\prime}(y) \)
    \( x + g^{\prime}(y) = x - 2y \)
  4. Solve for \( g(y) \):
    \( g^{\prime}(y) = -2y \implies g(y) = -y^2 \)
  5. Final Solution:
    \( x^2 + xy - y^2 = C \)

Example 3: Exponential Functions

Solve: \( (e^y + 2x) dx + (x e^y - 1) dy = 0 \)

  1. Test for Exactness:
    \( M_y = \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(e^y + 2x) = e^y \)
    \( N_x = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(x e^y - 1) = e^y \)
    Exact!
  2. Integrate \( M \) w.r.t \( x \):
    \( F(x,y) = \int (e^y + 2x) dx = x e^y + x^2 + g(y) \)
  3. Equate \( F_y \) to \( N \):
    \( F_y = x e^y + g^{\prime}(y) \)
    \( x e^y + g^{\prime}(y) = x e^y - 1 \)
  4. Solve for \( g(y) \):
    \( g^{\prime}(y) = -1 \implies g(y) = -y \)
  5. Final Solution:
    \( x e^y + x^2 - y = C \)

Example 4: Trigonometric Functions

Solve: \( (\cos x \cos y + 2x) dx - (\sin x \sin y + 2y) dy = 0 \)

  1. Test for Exactness:
    \( M_y = -\cos x \sin y \)
    \( N_x = -\cos x \sin y \) (Note the negative sign belongs to N)
    Exact!
  2. Integrate \( M \) w.r.t \( x \):
    \( F(x,y) = \int (\cos x \cos y + 2x) dx = \sin x \cos y + x^2 + g(y) \)
  3. Equate \( F_y \) to \( N \):
    \( F_y = -\sin x \sin y + g^{\prime}(y) \)
    \( -\sin x \sin y + g^{\prime}(y) = -\sin x \sin y - 2y \)
  4. Solve for \( g(y) \):
    \( g^{\prime}(y) = -2y \implies g(y) = -y^2 \)
  5. Final Solution:
    \( \sin x \cos y + x^2 - y^2 = C \)

Example 5: Logarithms & Fractions

Solve: \( (\frac{y}{x}) dx + (\ln x + 2y) dy = 0 \), for \( x > 0 \)

  1. Test for Exactness:
    \( M_y = \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(\frac{y}{x}) = \frac{1}{x} \)
    \( N_x = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(\ln x + 2y) = \frac{1}{x} \)
    Exact!
  2. Integrate \( N \) w.r.t \( y \) (Alternative approach!):
    Sometimes integrating N is easier.
    \( F(x,y) = \int (\ln x + 2y) dy = y \ln x + y^2 + h(x) \)
  3. Equate \( F_x \) to \( M \):
    \( F_x = \frac{y}{x} + h^{\prime}(x) \)
    \( \frac{y}{x} + h^{\prime}(x) = \frac{y}{x} \)
  4. Solve for \( h(x) \):
    \( h^{\prime}(x) = 0 \implies h(x) = 0 \)
  5. Final Solution:
    \( y \ln x + y^2 = C \)

Example 6: Initial Value Problem

Solve: \( y^2 dx + (2xy - 3) dy = 0 \), given \( y(1) = 2 \)

  1. Test for Exactness:
    \( M_y = 2y \), \( N_x = 2y \). Exact!
  2. Find F(x,y):
    \( F(x,y) = \int y^2 dx = xy^2 + g(y) \)
    \( F_y = 2xy + g^{\prime}(y) = 2xy - 3 \implies g^{\prime}(y) = -3 \implies g(y) = -3y \)
  3. General Solution:
    \( xy^2 - 3y = C \)
  4. Apply Initial Condition: Plug in \( x=1, y=2 \)
    \( (1)(2)^2 - 3(2) = C \)
    \( 4 - 6 = C \implies C = -2 \)
  5. Particular Solution:
    \( xy^2 - 3y = -2 \)
