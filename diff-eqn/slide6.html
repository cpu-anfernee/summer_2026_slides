Chapter 2.6

Homogeneous Equations

Solving First-Order DEs using Substitution

What is a Homogeneous Equation?

A first-order differential equation is homogeneous if it can be written in the form:

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} = f(x,y) \]

where \( f(x,y) \) can be expressed strictly as a function of the ratio \( \frac{y}{x} \).

The Alternative Test

An equation in differential form \( M(x,y)dx + N(x,y)dy = 0 \) is homogeneous if both \( M \) and \( N \) are homogeneous functions of the same degree. This means:

\[ M(tx, ty) = t^n M(x,y) \quad \text{and} \quad N(tx, ty) = t^n N(x,y) \]

The Substitution Method

Homogeneous equations are not separable as written, but they can be transformed into separable equations using an algebraic substitution.

  1. Substitute: Let \( y = vx \). (This implies that \( v = \frac{y}{x} \)).
  2. Differentiate: Using the Product Rule, find \( dy \):
    \[ dy = v \, dx + x \, dv \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{dy}{dx} = v + x\frac{dv}{dx} \]
  3. Transform: Replace all instances of \( y \) and \( dy \) in the original equation. The equation will miraculously simplify into a separable equation in terms of \( v \) and \( x \).
  4. Integrate: Separate the variables \( v \) and \( x \), then integrate both sides.
  5. Back-Substitute: Replace \( v \) with \( \frac{y}{x} \) to state the final solution in terms of the original variables.

Example 1: Direct Ratio Format

Solve: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x + y}{x} \)

  1. Rewrite the Equation: Split the fraction to reveal the \( y/x \) ratio.
    \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 1 + \frac{y}{x} \)
  2. Substitute: Let \( y = vx \), so \( \frac{dy}{dx} = v + x\frac{dv}{dx} \).
    \( v + x\frac{dv}{dx} = 1 + v \)
  3. Simplify and Separate: The \( v \) terms cancel out!
    \( x\frac{dv}{dx} = 1 \implies dv = \frac{dx}{x} \)
  4. Integrate:
    \( \int dv = \int \frac{dx}{x} \implies v = \ln|x| + C \)
  5. Back-Substitute: Replace \( v \) with \( y/x \).
    \( \frac{y}{x} = \ln|x| + C \implies y = x\ln|x| + Cx \)

Example 2: Differential Form

Solve: \( (x^2 + y^2)dx - 2xy \, dy = 0 \)

  1. Substitute: Let \( y = vx \) and \( dy = v\,dx + x\,dv \).
    \( (x^2 + v^2x^2)dx - 2x(vx)(v\,dx + x\,dv) = 0 \)
  2. Factor and Simplify: Divide the entire equation by \( x^2 \).
    \( (1 + v^2)dx - 2v(v\,dx + x\,dv) = 0 \)
  3. Distribute and Group \( dx \) and \( dv \):
    \( dx + v^2dx - 2v^2dx - 2vx\,dv = 0 \)
    \( (1 - v^2)dx = 2vx\,dv \)
  4. Separate Variables:
    \( \frac{dx}{x} = \frac{2v}{1 - v^2} dv \)
  5. Integrate: Use \( u \)-substitution on the right (\( u = 1 - v^2 \)).
    \( \ln|x| = -\ln|1 - v^2| + C_1 \)
  6. Back-Substitute and Solve:
    \( \ln|x| + \ln|1 - v^2| = C_1 \implies \ln|x(1 - v^2)| = C_1 \implies x(1 - v^2) = C \)
    \( x(1 - \frac{y^2}{x^2}) = C \implies x^2 - y^2 = Cx \)

Example 3: Exponential Homogeneity

Solve: \( x \frac{dy}{dx} = y + x e^{y/x} \)

  1. Rewrite the Equation: Divide by \( x \) to expose the ratio.
    \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y}{x} + e^{y/x} \)
  2. Substitute: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = v + x\frac{dv}{dx} \)
    \( v + x\frac{dv}{dx} = v + e^v \)
  3. Simplify and Separate: Subtract \( v \) from both sides.
    \( x\frac{dv}{dx} = e^v \implies \frac{dv}{e^v} = \frac{dx}{x} \implies e^{-v} dv = \frac{dx}{x} \)
  4. Integrate:
    \( -e^{-v} = \ln|x| + C \)
  5. Back-Substitute:
    \( -e^{-y/x} = \ln|x| + C \)

Example 4: Rational Degrees

Solve: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y^2 + xy}{x^2} \)

  1. Rewrite the Equation: Split the fraction.
    \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \left(\frac{y}{x}\right)^2 + \frac{y}{x} \)
  2. Substitute: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = v + x\frac{dv}{dx} \)
    \( v + x\frac{dv}{dx} = v^2 + v \)
  3. Simplify and Separate: Subtract \( v \).
    \( x\frac{dv}{dx} = v^2 \implies \frac{dv}{v^2} = \frac{dx}{x} \implies v^{-2} dv = \frac{dx}{x} \)
  4. Integrate: Apply the Power Rule.
    \( -v^{-1} = \ln|x| + C \implies -\frac{1}{v} = \ln|x| + C \)
  5. Back-Substitute: Replace \( v \) with \( y/x \).
    \( -\frac{x}{y} = \ln|x| + C \implies y = \frac{-x}{\ln|x| + C} \)

Example 5: Radical Homogeneity

Solve: \( (y + \sqrt{x^2 + y^2})dx - x \, dy = 0 \), for \( x > 0 \)

  1. Substitute: Let \( y = vx \) and \( dy = v\,dx + x\,dv \).
    \( (vx + \sqrt{x^2 + v^2x^2})dx - x(v\,dx + x\,dv) = 0 \)
  2. Factor the Root: Since \( x > 0 \), \( \sqrt{x^2(1+v^2)} = x\sqrt{1+v^2} \).
    \( (vx + x\sqrt{1+v^2})dx - xv\,dx - x^2dv = 0 \)
  3. Divide by \( x \) and Group:
    \( (v + \sqrt{1+v^2})dx - v\,dx - x\,dv = 0 \)
    \( \sqrt{1+v^2}dx = x\,dv \implies \frac{dx}{x} = \frac{dv}{\sqrt{1+v^2}} \)
  4. Integrate: The right side is a standard integration formula.
    \( \ln|x| = \ln|v + \sqrt{1+v^2}| + C_1 \)
  5. Back-Substitute:
    \( \ln|x| = \ln\left|\frac{y}{x} + \sqrt{1 + \frac{y^2}{x^2}}\right| + C_1 \)
    (Can be algebraically simplified further, but this implicit form is acceptable).

Example 6: Initial Value Problem

Solve: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y}{x} + \frac{x}{y} \), given \( y(1) = 2 \)

  1. Substitute: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = v + x\frac{dv}{dx} \)
    \( v + x\frac{dv}{dx} = v + \frac{1}{v} \)
  2. Simplify and Separate:
    \( x\frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{1}{v} \implies v\,dv = \frac{dx}{x} \)
  3. Integrate:
    \( \frac{v^2}{2} = \ln|x| + C \implies \frac{y^2}{2x^2} = \ln|x| + C \)
  4. General Solution: Multiply by \( 2x^2 \).
    \( y^2 = 2x^2(\ln|x| + C) \)
  5. Apply Initial Condition: Plug in \( x = 1 \) and \( y = 2 \).
    \( (2)^2 = 2(1)^2(\ln(1) + C) \implies 4 = 2(0 + C) \implies C = 2 \)
    Particular Solution: \( y^2 = 2x^2(\ln|x| + 2) \)
